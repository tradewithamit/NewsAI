U.S. consumer sentiment falls in September as job and inflation worries rise

Inflation and tariffs worry Americans Year-ahead inflation expectations stayed at 4.8%, same as in August. Many Americans are anxious about tariffs ‚Äî 60% of survey participants mentioned tariffs without being asked. Worries about jobs are rising ‚Äî about 65% of consumers expect higher unemployment in the next 12 months. This is higher than last month, when around 60% expected worse employment conditions, a level last seen during the Great Recession. The consumer survey follows weak economic data in recent days, showing troubling trends in jobs and inflation. Payrolls dropped by 13,000 in June, the first net loss since December 2020, as per US Labor Department. Job market shows weak signs Earlier payroll data for the past year was revised down ‚Äî the US had 911,000 fewer jobs than initially reported. New York Fed survey shows low confidence in finding new jobs ‚Äî Americans think chances of getting a job after losing one are the lowest since June 2013, as stated by Yahoo Finance.
